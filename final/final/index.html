Privacy-Preserving Data Publishing

The information age has enabled many organizations to gather large volumes of data. However, the usefulness of this data is negligible if “meaningful information” or “knowledge” cannot be extracted from it. Data mining, otherwise known as knowledge discovery (KDD), attempts to answer this need. A key problem that arises in any mass collection of data is that of confidentiality. The need for privacy is sometimes due to law (e.g., for medical databases) or can be motivated by business interests. For this we use Privacy-preserving Techniques to ensure privacy to the published micro data. Data Anonymization is a technique that converts the clear text into non-human readable form. These techniques are getting lot of attention over the past few years for privacy-preserving data publishing. Data Anonymization allows the transfer of information across two departments in same agency or different agencies while reducing the risk of disclosure of sensitive information. The most popular data anonymization techniques are Generalization (k-anonymity), Bucketization (l-diversity). There is one more variation of Generalization namely Multiset-based Generalization. In this project we will see how existing system uses these techniques to publish the micro data, and how these publish micro data suffers from various attach that can compromise the privacy of the micro data, and how my proposed algorithm can overcome the flaws of his predecessor’s and improve the existing system
